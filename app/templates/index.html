Welcome to acoustic radiation force calculator!

  • On this page you can calculate acoustic radiation force from arbitrary pressure field, acting on elastic sphere of any size.
  • Calculation technique is described in a paper.
  • If you want to use a field of focused source, you can calculate it on a tab Create Model. Enter parameters of the source and press “Calculate”.
  • If you want to use an arbitrary field, you can load .mat-file with acoustic pressure field in square table format on a tab Load Model. Pressure must be complex and contain information about amplitude and phase in every point. Also enter name of your model and another important data (special step, amplitude factor, etc.) about your field.
  • The field will be saved on a tab Models.
  • Then enter parameters of the scatterer and coordinates, where it is positioned. If you want to calculate radiation force along the only one axis, enter same “start” and “end” values at other axis. Pay attention on chosen parameters of scatterer and amount of points: in case of big scatterer calculation can take significant amount of time.
  • Results of radiation force calculation are also saved on a tab Models. You can download a .txt-file with results.

Geometry of source and scatterer

  • The surface of the sound wave source is located on the S0 plane. The Rayleigh integral is used to calculate the distribution of pressure and phase on the plane S, located perpendicular to the source at a certain distance from it.
  • A coordinate system is introduced with respect to the pressure distribution on the S plane: x and y are located in the S plane, and z is directed perpendicularly.
  • The acoustic radiation force acting on a given scatterer placed at a point with coordinates (x, y, z) is calculated.
